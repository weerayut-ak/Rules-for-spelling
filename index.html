1. The Eight Rules of the Silent “E”

How Do You Use the Silent 'E'_ _ StoryBots_ Learn to Read _ Netflix Jr.mp4

🛑 Prevents words from ending in V or U

🎵 No words end in v, so we add a silent e

Example: love, live, cave, save, brave

🎵 No words end in u, so we add a silent e

Example: blue, clue, due, cue

✨ “No words end in V or U, add an E—that’s what we do!”

🎩 The Magic E where its makes the vowel says its name

When an “e” comes around a consonant, make the vowel says its name.

The pattern goes like this: ( vowel consonant and the letter e )

for example: bake, cake, like, bike, coke

✨ “Magic E at the end, makes the vowel say its name, my friend!”

🧊 Silent E keeps C and G soft

What it does: Silent e helps keep:

  • c = /s/ (face, rice)
  • g = /j/ (age, huge)

Without “e”:

  • cap (k sound) vs cape
  • rag (g sound) vs rage
Teaching tip: This is super important for pronunciation.
✨ “C says /s/ and G says /j/, Silent E keeps them that way!”

👯 Shows word meaning (homophones)

What it does: Silent e helps distinguish words that sound the same:

by / bye be / bee or / ore
Teaching tip: Great for vocabulary building
✨ “Same sound, different way— Silent E helps all the way!”

🔄 Silent E can signal a word change (Special Rule)

Sometimes adding a silent e changes both the sound and the meaning of a word.

Examples:

bath → bathe
breath → breathe
cloth → clothe
teeth → teethe

Pattern:

  • Left side = usually noun (a thing)
  • Right side = usually verb (an action)

🔊 Sound change:

  • th changes from soft /θ/ → voiced /ð/
  • Sometimes the vowel sound changes too

⚠️ Important note:

This is a special pattern, not a general rule.

❌ It is:

  • Not always true
  • Not easy to predict

🚫 Examples that do NOT follow the pattern:

  • both → (no bothe)
  • math → (no mathe)
✨ “Add an E, the sound may change—from soft to voiced, a little strange!”

2. CVC Doubling Rule (Double the consonant)

Rule: When a word has:

  • 1 syllable
  • Consonant–Vowel–Consonant (CVC)

👉 Double the last consonant before adding -ing, -ed

Examples:

run → running
sit → sitting
hop → hopped

3. Drop the “E” Rule

🎥 Video Lesson: Drop the E

Rule: If a word ends in silent e, drop the e before adding -ing

Examples:

make → making
write → writing
dance → dancing
BUT: There are exceptions to the rule: see → seeing (keep e)
✨ “Silent E, goodbye for now—add -ing, drop it out!”
