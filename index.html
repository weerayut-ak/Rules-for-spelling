🔇 Rule 1: The Eight Rules of Silent "E"

Silent E has many important jobs in English spelling!

📌 1.1 – Prevents Words Ending in V or U

English words don't end in the letters V or U. A silent E is added to the end to fix this!

🚫 No words end in V:

love live cave save brave

🚫 No words end in U:

blue clue due cue

🎵 "No words end in V or U, add an E—that's what we do!"

✨ 1.2 – Magic E: Makes the Vowel Say Its Name

When a word follows the pattern Vowel – Consonant – E, the "E" makes the vowel say its long sound (its name)!

Vowel → Consonant → E

The vowel says its name! 🎤

bake cake like bike coke

🎵 "Magic E at the end, makes the vowel say its name, my friend!"

🔤 1.3 – Silent E Keeps C and G Soft

Silent E helps keep C sounding like /s/ and G sounding like /j/.

C = /s/ sound:

face rice
Without E: cap /k/ → cape /k…/ same but face has /s/!

G = /j/ sound:

age huge
Without E: rag /g/ → rage /j/

🌱 Teaching Tip:

This is super important for pronunciation! Remove the E and the sound changes.

🎵 "C says /s/ and G says /j/, Silent E keeps them that way!"

📖 1.4 – Shows Word Meaning (Homophones)

Silent E helps us tell apart words that sound the same (homophones)!

by

↕️

bye

be

↕️

bee

or

↕️

ore

🌱 Teaching Tip:

Great for vocabulary building! Use both words in sentences to show the difference.

🎵 "Same sound, different way— Silent E helps all the way!"

⚡ 1.5 – Silent E Can Signal a Word Change (Special Rule)

Sometimes adding a silent E changes both the sound and the meaning — often from a noun to a verb!

Noun → Verb examples:

bath → bathe
breath → breathe
cloth → clothe
teeth → teethe

🔊 Sound change:

The "th" changes from soft /θ/ → voiced /ð/. Sometimes the vowel sound changes too!

⚠️ Exceptions (no pattern):

• both → (no "bothe")

• math → (no "mathe")

🎵 "Add an E, the sound may change—from soft to voiced, a little strange!"

📝 Rule 2: CVC Doubling Rule

Double the last consonant before adding -ing or -ed!

When a word has:

1️⃣

1 Syllable

🔤

C–V–C Pattern

➕

Adding -ing or -ed

👉 Double the last consonant!

Examples with -ing:

run → runnning
sit → sitting

Examples with -ed:

hop → hopped

🎵 "One syllable, C-V-C—double the last letter, can't you see!"

✂️ Rule 3: Drop the "E" Rule

If a word ends in silent E, drop it before adding -ing!

Word ends in silent E?

✂️ + ing

Drop the E, then add -ing!

make

⬇️

drop the E

making
write

⬇️

drop the E

writing
dance

⬇️

drop the E

dancing

⚠️ Exception — Keep the E:

see → seeing (keep the E!)

🎵 "Silent E, goodbye for now—add -ing, drop it out!"

🅸 Rule 4: I Before E Rule

The classic spelling rule—with a famous exception!

I before E,

except after C

...but sometimes it's different, you'll see! 🙃

✅ Follow the Rule:

believe receive field piece

⚠️ Exceptions (many exist!):

weird science their

🌱 Teaching Note:

This rule has MANY exceptions! It's helpful, but not always right. Teach it as a starting point, not a guarantee!

🎵 "I before E, except after C—but sometimes it's different, you'll see!"

👑 Rule 5: The "QU" Rule

Q and U always go together — they're best friends!

Q 🤝 U

Q is almost always followed by U!

Examples:

queen quick question quiet quilt squeeze

🌱 Teaching Tip:

Tell students: "If you see Q, look for its partner U right after it! They're always together in English words."

🎵 "Q and U go hand in hand— together they always stand!"
